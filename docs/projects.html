Communication (Writing, Editing, Publishing)

üñä

WHAT: I am open to commissions for writing and editing projects, including but not limited to: (popular) science writing, technical writing, web copywriting, sports writing, human interest features and profiles, reviews. Given my extensive experience in covering diverse topics, I can adapt to a broad range of styles and needs, whether news or longform features or in-depth analyses, for specialized or public audiences.

WHY: My passion for storytelling began as early as 4th grade, and I have been writing ever since. I now have 3+ years of experience as a university campus journalist, including serving as Editor-in-Chief; 1 year as a staff writer for a Singapore-based science media agency, where I also engaged in editing and project management duties; and 13 months and counting as a freelance writer. Attention to detail is a key strength of mine, enabling swift detection of errors or inconsistencies, whether in my own drafts or when proofreading print or digital copies before they are unveiled to the public eye. Check out my Portfolio for sample works and style references.

HOW & WHEN: If you are interested in working together, shoot me an email. At least a two-week lead time is requested for new projects, and I would especially recommend a longer advance notice if the direction of the story (i.e., angle or focus points, sources/interviewees) needs to be decided. Exceptions include a sufficiently detailed project brief or provision of existing materials and resources. For previous clients, a one-week lead time is still preferrable for standard article lengths (1000-2000 words). I strive to respond as promptly as possible, but please bear in mind that I am a full-time graduate student and spend long hours in the lab.

Proofreading and line editing or copyediting would cost around 70% of the fees for writing a piece of the same length, while structural and developmental editing will cost 85-100% of the rate. Rush projects, including one-day turnarounds for revisions, and complete rewrites will incur additional fees.

Small tokens of appreciation are always welcome, but I typically do not charge for such outreach activities, especially from non-profits and youth organizations.
