Erinne Cherisse Ong

Scientist & Writer

connecting cancer, (brain) circuits, and cognition

💡

Broadly, I seek to investigate the regulation of signal transduction and cell death pathways in cancer biology and neuroscience. I am fascinated by neuroplasticity—the intricate mechanisms underlying synaptic transmission and how experiences reshape the wiring of brain circuits. I am also interested in the integration of tumors in synaptic networks and the effects on memory.

After my stint as a campus journalist, I have covered science and technology advances across Asia for a Singapore-based STEM media company. I strive to deliver powerful and engaging writing that disentangles the complex dynamics of systems, captures the cumulative and experimental nature of science, and sheds light on contemporary issues in science and society.

I will soon pursue postgraduate training at the University of Göttingen International Max Planck Research School for Neurosciences in Germany. Ultimately, I envision fostering science literacy in the Philippines—with the belief that societal progress rests in part on effective science communication and using research data as evidence to support context-specific interventions for improving human health and welfare.
